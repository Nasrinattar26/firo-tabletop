Tabletop Reservoir Management Exercise
Overview
This project simulates a reservoir management decision-making exercise from May through September. Users act as reservoir operators and make monthly release decisions under uncertainty.

Reservoir Specifications
Total Capacity: 13 units
Conservation Pool: 5 units
Flood Pool: 8 units
FIRO Pool Limit: 6 units
Operating Strategies
1. No FIRO (Current Guide Curve)
Maintain storage at 5 units
Focus on meeting demand with minimal retained storage
2. CIRO (Climatology-Based)
Retain up to 6 units during June–August
Uses historical inflow patterns
3. FIRO (Forecast-Based)
Uses ensemble streamflow forecasts
Forecast percentiles: 10%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 90%
Adjust releases based on expected inflow conditions
Gameplay Mechanics
Start in May with:

Initial storage
Demand
Evaporation
Each month:

Choose a release amount
Decision is final
After submission:

Actual inflow is revealed
Storage is updated
Repeat through September

Constraints
Flooding (Surcharge)
Storage must not exceed 13 units
Excess causes spillway release and penalty
Deficit
Storage below 5 units results in water shortage penalties
Scoring System
1. Surcharge Penalty
+50 points if no surcharge
0 points if any surcharge occurs
2. Cumulative Deficit
Total deficit (below 5 units) is subtracted
3. Storage Bonus
Credit for storage up to 6 units
Storage above 6 units earns no additional benefit
Scenarios
The exercise includes three historical scenarios:

2012
2016
2017
Each scenario is implemented in spreadsheet tabs.

Objective
Maximize total score by balancing:

Flood risk
Water supply reliability
Strategic storage
Notes
Decisions are irreversible
Forecast uncertainty plays a key role
Strategy adaptation is encouraged
